Evidence Quality
Evidence Analysis
Evidence quality gaps observed in pilot submissions, and parameters ranked by evidence availability.
Key finding: Most schools submitted some evidence, but quality was inconsistent. A large share showed existence-based proof rather than usage or systems-based evidence. Competitive exam, FLN, and POSH parameters had the lowest evidence submission rates.
Six Evidence Gap Patterns
01
Single Instance Evidence
Observed: One sample used to represent entire school
Example: One classroom photo for lighting
Why it fails: Does not show school-wide compliance
What's needed: Photos of 4–5 classrooms + summary note
02
Existence vs. Usage
Observed: Infrastructure shown, no functional proof
Example: Library shelf photo submitted
Why it fails: No proof of active use
What's needed: Issue register + timetable + usage logs
03
Event-Based Proof
Observed: One-time activity shown as routine
Example: Single PTM photo submitted
Why it fails: Doesn't show frequency or regularity
What's needed: PTM calendar + attendance from 2–3 PTMs
04
Infrastructure Without Functionality
Observed: Asset present, operational status unknown
Example: Computer lab photo submitted
Why it fails: Equipment could be non-functional
What's needed: Timetable + maintenance log + student usage data
05
Fragmented Documentation
Observed: Register page without continuity
Example: Single page image from register
Why it fails: No proof of regular maintenance
What's needed: Cover page + dated entries across multiple weeks
06
Display Compliance
Observed: Posters shown as systems compliance
Example: POCSO poster photo submitted
Why it fails: No committee or system evidence
What's needed: Committee order + meeting minutes + training records

Thematic Analysis of Open-Ended Responses
Photo Upload Problems~8 mentions

Server failures, file size limits (max 5 photos), compression issues. Most common technical complaint across all responses. Came up in Q3, Q7, Q8.

Missing "NA" Option~7 mentions

Primary schools have no guard, counsellor, science lab. Single-gender schools can't answer gender parity questions. No way to mark "Not Applicable" for many parameters. Q6, Q7, Q8.

Parameters Irrelevant for Primary Schools~6 mentions

Guard, counsellor, non-teaching staff, science lab, ICT lab, competitive exams. These structures don't exist in parishadiya primary schools. Q6.

Network Issues in Rural Areas~5 mentions

Forms timing out and going back mid-fill due to poor connectivity. Especially in villages. One school suggested offline-first, then online. Q7.

FLN and Assessment Evidence Hard to Produce~4 mentions

FLN reading/numeracy results, competitive exam data, pedagogy evidence, and behavioural records were hardest to locate and upload. Q3.

Want Pre-Training or Offline Preview~4 mentions

Schools wanted a training session before filling. One suggested giving the form as a PDF first so evidence can be collected before the online window opens. Q5, Q8.

Options Were "Clubbed" (Combined)~3 mentions

Multiple conditions packed into single options made it hard to select the right level. Schools wanted more granular choices, or 5 options instead of 3. Q3, Q7, Q8.

UDISE+ Data Hard to Extract~3 mentions

Schools struggled to pull DCF from UDISE+. Evidence format was unclear. Some didn't know how to generate the required reports. Q3, Q7.

Time Pressure During Board Exam Season~3 mentions

50% said time was "just manageable." The pilot ran during board exam prep. Schools were also exam centres. Better timing would improve quality. Q4, Q7.

Want Remarks/Comment Box Per Domain~2 mentions

Schools wanted space to explain context. Current form only allows selecting options, with no room for nuance or explanation. Q5, Q8.

Positive Sentiment~4 mentions

Multiple schools called SQAAF valuable. One principal wrote it re-energised focus on forgotten aspects. Another thanked for the self-assessment opportunity. Q7, Q8.
